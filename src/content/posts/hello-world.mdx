This is the first post on **exitcondition** — my personal blog, rebuilt from
scratch on the same stack that runs the Aileron documentation site: Astro 6,
Svelte 5, and Tailwind v4, with the shared design tokens that drive the rest
of the family of sites.

## Why "exitcondition"

In programming, an *exit condition* is the rule that decides when a loop
stops. Every loop needs one. So does every project, every career chapter,
and every late-night thought.

This blog is where I'll write about the ones I've crossed, the ones I'm
chasing, and the ones I haven't quite figured out yet.

## What you'll find here

A few topics I expect to circle back to:

- Open source and the long tail of running a project past launch
- Financial technology — what makes the boring parts actually hard
- The shape of an engineering career, told without the brochure voice
- Notes from the workbench: tools, patterns, and the occasional dead end

## Colophon

The site is built on Astro with content authored in MDX, styled with
Tailwind v4 against a token system shared with the Aileron documentation
stack. The left sidebar lists every post grouped by year. The textured
page background is a generated SVG tile, parallax-scrolled at half rate.
